# Quiz: Sanctions Screening

---

**Q1. OFAC stands for:**

A) Office of Financial AML Controls  
B) Office of Foreign Assets Control  
C) Organization for Financial Audit and Compliance  
D) Office of Federal Anti-Crime

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Office of Foreign Assets Control.** OFAC is the US Treasury Department body that administers and enforces US economic and trade sanctions.

</details>

---

**Q2. Sanctions compliance is typically considered:**

A) A best-efforts obligation  
B) Strict liability — violations can occur even without knowledge or intent  
C) Only applicable to US-headquartered institutions  
D) Discretionary based on transaction value

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Strict liability.** Unlike many AML obligations, sanctions violations can result in penalties even where there was no intent or knowledge of the violation — making robust screening and controls essential.

</details>

---

**Q3. Under OFAC's 50% Rule, an entity is considered blocked if:**

A) A sanctioned person owns exactly 50% of it  
B) One or more sanctioned persons own 50% or more in aggregate, even if not separately listed  
C) It conducts more than 50% of its business with sanctioned countries  
D) A sanctioned person is a director but owns no shares

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) One or more sanctioned persons own 50% or more in aggregate, even if not separately listed.** The 50% Rule aggregates ownership across multiple sanctioned persons, meaning an entity with two 30% sanctioned owners (60% total) is blocked even though neither individually crosses 50%.

</details>

---

**Q4. A USD transaction creates OFAC nexus even for non-US institutions because:**

A) All global banks must comply with US law regardless of location  
B) USD payments typically clear through US correspondent banks  
C) OFAC has direct enforcement authority in all countries  
D) The dollar is the world's reserve currency

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) USD payments typically clear through US correspondent banks.** When funds move through US correspondent banks to settle USD transactions, those transactions fall within OFAC's jurisdiction — giving OFAC reach into transactions processed by foreign institutions.

</details>

---

**Q5. The SDN List is maintained by:**

A) The UN Security Council  
B) The EU Council  
C) OFAC (US Treasury)  
D) Interpol

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) OFAC (US Treasury).** The Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List is OFAC's primary sanctions list. The UN maintains its own Consolidated List; the EU and UK maintain separate lists.

</details>

---

**Q6. When a screening hit has a 65% match score and no matching date of birth, you should:**

A) Clear it immediately — below 70% is always a false positive  
B) Treat it as a confirmed true match  
C) Request additional identifying information before making a determination  
D) File a SAR immediately without further investigation

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Request additional identifying information before making a determination.** A partial match without sufficient confirming identifiers should not be cleared without attempting to obtain additional information (DOB, passport number, address) to confirm or rule out the match.

</details>

---

**Q7. "Sectoral sanctions" means:**

A) The entire country is comprehensively sanctioned  
B) Specific sectors of an economy (e.g., energy, defense) face restrictions, not the full country  
C) Only government officials are sanctioned  
D) Sanctions apply only to transactions above a certain value

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Specific sectors of an economy face restrictions, not the full country.** Sectoral sanctions (e.g., Russia's energy and defense sectors) target specific industries rather than imposing comprehensive country-wide restrictions.

</details>

---

**Q8. After confirming a true sanctions match, your first action should be:**

A) Call the customer to inform them  
B) File a SAR immediately and wait for instructions  
C) Block/freeze the transaction or account and escalate to compliance immediately  
D) Continue processing but flag for review

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Block/freeze the transaction or account and escalate to compliance immediately.** Confirmed sanctions matches require immediate action — blocking processing and freezing assets (where applicable) — followed by regulatory reporting. Informing the customer would constitute "tipping off."

</details>

---

**Q9. Which of the following is NOT one of the major sanctions regimes?**

A) OFAC (USA)  
B) UN Security Council Sanctions  
C) INTERPOL Red Notices  
D) OFSI (UK)

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) INTERPOL Red Notices.** INTERPOL Red Notices are international law enforcement alerts for wanted persons — they are not sanctions designations. OFAC, UN, and OFSI are all primary sanctions regimes.

</details>

---

**Q10. Person A (sanctioned) owns 30% of Company X. Person B (also sanctioned, unrelated to A) owns 25% of Company X. Is Company X blocked under the 50% Rule?**

A) No — neither individual alone reaches 50%  
B) Yes — aggregate sanctioned ownership is 55%, exceeding the 50% threshold  
C) Only if Company X is registered in a sanctioned country  
D) Only Person A's 30% stake is blocked; the company itself is not

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Yes — aggregate sanctioned ownership is 55%, exceeding the 50% threshold.** The 50% Rule aggregates ownership across all sanctioned persons regardless of whether they are related to each other. 30% + 25% = 55% → Company X is blocked.

</details>
