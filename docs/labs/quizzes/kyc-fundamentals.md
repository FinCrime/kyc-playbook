# Quiz: KYC Fundamentals

---

**Q1. What does CIP stand for and what is its purpose?**

- A) Customer Identity Protocol — verifying transactions
- B) Customer Identification Programme — verifying who the customer is
- C) Compliance Investigation Process — investigating suspicious accounts
- D) Corporate Identity Programme — verifying business entities

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Customer Identification Programme — verifying who the customer is.** CIP is the foundational KYC process for confirming customer identity before establishing a business relationship.

</details>

---

**Q2. Which three elements form the complete KYC framework?**

- A) CIP, CDD, SAR Filing
- B) CDD, EDD, Transaction Monitoring
- C) CIP, CDD, Ongoing Monitoring
- D) KYB, UBO, EDD

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) CIP, CDD, Ongoing Monitoring.** CIP establishes identity; CDD assesses risk and purpose; Ongoing Monitoring ensures the relationship remains consistent over time.

</details>

---

**Q3. For a utility bill to be acceptable as proof of address, it should typically be:**

- A) Within 6 months
- B) Within 3 months
- C) Within 12 months
- D) Any date — address documents don't expire for KYC purposes

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Within 3 months.** Most KYC policies require proof of address documents to be no older than 3 months to ensure currency of the information.

</details>

---

**Q4. The standard UBO ownership threshold in most jurisdictions is:**

- A) 10%
- B) 51%
- C) 25%
- D) 33%

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) 25%.** The 25% ownership threshold (direct or indirect) is the standard UBO identification trigger under FATF guidance, EU AML Directives, UK PSC rules, and the US Corporate Transparency Act.

</details>

---

**Q5. Which of the following customers would automatically require EDD?**

- A) A salaried employee opening a savings account
- B) A publicly listed company seeking a corporate account
- C) A Politically Exposed Person opening a private banking relationship
- D) A freelancer with a good credit score

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) A Politically Exposed Person.** PEP status is an automatic EDD trigger regardless of other risk factors. Publicly listed companies may qualify for SDD due to their disclosure obligations.

</details>

---

**Q6. What is the difference between Source of Funds and Source of Wealth?**

- A) They mean the same thing — both refer to how the customer earned their money
- B) SoF = origin of specific transaction funds; SoW = how the customer's overall net worth was accumulated
- C) SoF = required for all customers; SoW = only required for EDD
- D) SoF is for individuals; SoW is for businesses

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) SoF = origin of specific transaction funds; SoW = how the customer's overall net worth was accumulated.** SoW is broader and typically required for PEPs and high-net-worth individuals.

</details>

---

**Q7. "Liveness detection" in biometric verification is designed to prevent:**

- A) ID document forgery
- B) Using a photo, video, or deepfake instead of a real person
- C) Database errors during identity verification
- D) Name mismatches in screening results

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Using a photo, video, or deepfake instead of a real person.** Liveness detection confirms the person presenting is physically present, preventing spoofing attacks using static or video representations.

</details>

---

**Q8. Simplified Due Diligence (SDD) means:**

- A) No due diligence is required
- B) Identity verification is skipped
- C) Reduced (but not eliminated) due diligence proportionate to lower risk
- D) EDD with fewer documents

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Reduced (but not eliminated) due diligence proportionate to lower risk.** Identity verification is still required under SDD — it is the depth of additional information gathering and monitoring frequency that is reduced.

</details>

---

**Q9. KYC must be refreshed (outside scheduled periodic reviews) when:**

- A) The customer's balance exceeds a certain amount
- B) A material change occurs or suspicion arises
- C) The customer turns 65
- D) The customer has been with the institution for 5 years

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**B) A material change occurs or suspicion arises.** Event-driven triggers for KYC refresh include material changes in the customer's profile and the emergence of suspicious activity.

</details>

---

**Q10. For a trust structure, who must be identified during KYB/KYC?**

- A) Only the trustee
- B) Only the named beneficiaries
- C) The settlor, trustee(s), beneficiaries, and protector (if applicable)
- D) Only whoever signed the trust deed

<details>
<summary>Answer</summary>

**C) The settlor, trustee(s), beneficiaries, and protector (if applicable).** Comprehensive trust identification requires all relevant parties to be identified and screened, as any of them could ultimately control or benefit from the trust's assets.

</details>
