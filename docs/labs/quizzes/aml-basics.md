# Quiz: AML Basics

Test your knowledge of AML fundamentals. Try to answer each question before revealing the answer.

---

**Q1. Which stage of money laundering involves introducing criminal proceeds into the financial system?**

A) Integration  
B) Placement  
C) Layering  
D) Structuring

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Placement.** Placement is the first stage, where criminal proceeds (often cash) are introduced into the formal financial system. Structuring is a technique used during placement, not a stage itself.

</details>

---

**Q2. What is the primary purpose of the layering stage?**

A) Generate criminal proceeds  
B) Introduce cash into a bank  
C) Obscure the trail between funds and their criminal source  
D) Purchase real estate with clean money

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Obscure the trail between funds and their criminal source.** Layering creates complexity — multiple transactions, jurisdictions, and entities — designed to make tracing the source of funds as difficult as possible.

</details>

---

**Q3. Which FATF document sets the international AML standards that member countries must implement?**

A) The Basel Accords  
B) The 40 Recommendations  
C) The Wolfsberg Principles  
D) The Vienna Convention

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) The 40 Recommendations.** FATF's 40 Recommendations are the global standard for AML/CFT frameworks. The Wolfsberg Principles are industry guidance (banks), and Basel Accords cover capital adequacy, not AML specifically.

</details>

---

**Q4. Structuring (smurfing) is illegal:**

A) Only when the underlying funds are from criminal activity  
B) Only when the amounts exceed $5,000  
C) Even when the underlying funds are from a legitimate source  
D) Only if it involves multiple individuals

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Even when the underlying funds are from a legitimate source.** Under 31 U.S.C. § 5324, the intent to evade reporting requirements is itself the offense — source of funds is irrelevant to the structuring charge.

</details>

---

**Q5. A Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) should be filed when:**

A) A transaction exceeds $10,000 in cash  
B) There are reasonable grounds to suspect criminal activity, regardless of transaction size  
C) A customer fails identity verification  
D) A customer requests information about reporting thresholds

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) There are reasonable grounds to suspect criminal activity, regardless of transaction size.** SARs have no minimum dollar threshold — suspicion, not size, triggers the filing obligation. Cash transactions over $10,000 trigger a CTR (Currency Transaction Report), which is separate.

</details>

---

**Q6. Which of the following is a predicate offense for money laundering?**

A) Tax evasion  
B) Filing a SAR  
C) Conducting EDD  
D) Applying for a mortgage

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**A) Tax evasion.** Tax crimes are included in FATF's designated categories of predicate offenses. The others are compliance activities, not crimes.

</details>

---

**Q7. What does "tipping off" mean in an AML context?**

A) Providing a customer with information about another customer's account  
B) Informing a customer or third party that a SAR has been or may be filed  
C) Sharing SAR data between two financial institutions  
D) Filing a SAR without internal approval

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) Informing a customer or third party that a SAR has been or may be filed.** Tipping off is a criminal offense in most jurisdictions because it compromises the integrity of the investigation that may follow the SAR.

</details>

---

**Q8. The FATF "Grey List" refers to:**

A) Countries subject to comprehensive sanctions  
B) Countries on OFAC's SDN List  
C) Jurisdictions under increased monitoring for AML/CFT deficiencies  
D) Tax havens with limited financial transparency

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Jurisdictions under increased monitoring for AML/CFT deficiencies.** The Grey List (formally "Jurisdictions under Increased Monitoring") identifies countries actively working with FATF to address AML/CFT weaknesses. The Black List covers jurisdictions with the most severe deficiencies subject to a call for action.

</details>

---

**Q9. Which of the following is NOT typically a red flag for money laundering?**

A) Transactions just below currency reporting thresholds  
B) A customer who provides complete documentation promptly  
C) Rapid pass-through transactions with no business purpose  
D) Involvement of shell companies in multiple secrecy jurisdictions

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**B) A customer who provides complete documentation promptly.** Prompt, cooperative provision of complete documentation is a positive indicator. The others are all recognized ML red flags.

</details>

---

**Q10. EDD is required for:**

A) All new customers regardless of risk  
B) Only customers who fail identity verification  
C) Higher-risk customers, including PEPs and those from high-risk jurisdictions  
D) Customers who have filed bankruptcy in the past 5 years

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**C) Higher-risk customers, including PEPs and those from high-risk jurisdictions.** EDD applies based on assessed risk — it is not universal, and bankruptcy history alone is not typically an automatic EDD trigger (though it may factor into risk rating).

</details>
