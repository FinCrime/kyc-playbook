# Lab: SAR Narrative Builder

## How to Use This Lab

This lab gives you raw investigation facts and asks you to construct a SAR narrative. Draft your own narrative first, then compare against the model answer.

---

## Scenario Facts (The Raw Investigation)

**Customer:** Farida Yusupova, individual, Uzbek national, UK resident  
**Account type:** Personal current account  
**Occupation on file:** Retail sales assistant (declared salary: £22,000/year)  
**Account age:** 14 months

**Transaction activity flagged:**
- Between 01 March and 30 April, the account received 11 separate inbound transfers from 8 different individuals (none previously transacting with this account), totalling £43,200
- All 11 transfers occurred on weekdays between 09:00–11:00
- Within 24–48 hours of each receipt, 87% of funds were transferred out to a single overseas account in Uzbekistan (beneficiary: Alisher Trade LLC)
- Three transfers to Uzbekistan were round amounts (£5,000, £8,000, £10,000)
- The account's prior 12 months showed average monthly credits of £1,850 (salary credits) and no international transfers

**Customer contact attempt:**
- Branch relationship manager contacted Ms. Yusupova by phone. She stated the funds were "from friends helping her send money home." When asked about Alisher Trade LLC, she said it was "a family business" and became evasive, ending the call.

**Research conducted:**
- Alisher Trade LLC: not findable in any public Uzbek business registry searched
- No adverse media on Ms. Yusupova
- Sanctions/PEP screening: clear

---

## Your Task

Write a SAR narrative of 200–300 words using the Who/What/When/Where/Why framework.

---

## Model SAR Narrative

<details>
<summary>✅ Reveal Model Narrative</summary>

> **Suspicious Activity Report — Narrative**
>
> **Who:** Farida Yusupova (DOB: [XX/XX/XXXX], Uzbek national, UK resident), holder of Personal Current Account #XXXXXXXX. Ms. Yusupova's declared occupation is retail sales assistant with annual income of approximately £22,000.
>
> **What:** Between 01 March [YEAR] and 30 April [YEAR], the account received 11 inbound transfers from 8 previously unknown third-party individuals totalling £43,200 — approximately 23 months of the customer's declared salary in a 60-day period. Within 24–48 hours of each receipt, approximately 87% of funds were transferred to a single Uzbekistan-based beneficiary (Alisher Trade LLC). Three individual outbound transfers were round amounts (£5,000, £8,000, and £10,000). The pattern of rapid aggregation from multiple senders followed by near-immediate onward transfer is consistent with a money mule or informal value transfer typology.
>
> **When:** The activity occurred between 01 March and 30 April [YEAR], with inbound transfers consistently occurring weekday mornings (09:00–11:00).
>
> **Where:** Funds received into a UK-domiciled personal account; disbursed to Alisher Trade LLC in Uzbekistan. The beneficiary entity cannot be verified in available Uzbek business registries.
>
> **Why:** [Institution] considers this activity suspicious for the following reasons: (1) transaction volume is approximately 23x the customer's declared monthly income, (2) the rapid pass-through pattern with multiple unrelated senders is inconsistent with personal use, (3) the beneficiary entity is unverifiable, (4) when contacted, the customer provided an implausible explanation ("friends helping send money home") and became evasive when asked specifically about the beneficiary entity, and (5) the activity represents a complete departure from 12 months of consistent, low-value salary-only credits.
>
> [Institution] is filing this SAR on the grounds of reasonable suspicion of money laundering, potentially involving use of the account as part of a money mule network or informal hawala-style transfer arrangement.

</details>

## Self-Assessment Checklist

Compare your narrative against these criteria:

- [ ] Named the customer with key identifying details
- [ ] Stated the specific transaction amounts and dates (not approximate)
- [ ] Explained the departure from the prior 12-month baseline
- [ ] Addressed the customer contact and evasive response
- [ ] Connected facts to a recognized typology (mule / IVTS)
- [ ] Stated clearly why the institution considers the activity suspicious
- [ ] Avoided unsupported speculation
- [ ] Written in plain, clear language a non-specialist could follow
