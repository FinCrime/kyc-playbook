# Lab: EDD Investigation

## Scenario

**Customer:** Ms. Priya Venkataraman
**Nationality:** Indian
**Residence:** Singapore (PR holder)
**Occupation:** Director, Venkataraman Holdings Pte Ltd (Singapore)
**Relationship Requested:** Private banking — investment account
**Declared Net Worth:** SGD 4.2 million
**Declared Source of Wealth:** "Family business in textiles and real estate"

**EDD Trigger:** Customer flagged as a Domestic PEP — her father, Mr. R. Venkataraman, served as a State Minister of Commerce in Tamil Nadu, India from 2014–2019.

---

## Evidence Available

### 1. Identity Documents
- Singapore PR card and Indian passport — both verified, no inconsistencies
- Proof of Singapore address: lease agreement for condominium unit (SGD 8,500/month rent)

### 2. Beneficial Ownership
- Venkataraman Holdings Pte Ltd: 100% owned by Ms. Venkataraman, incorporated in Singapore in 2017
- ACRA (Singapore registry) shows one director: Ms. Venkataraman
- Business activity: "Investment holding and real estate advisory"

### 3. Source of Wealth Documentation Provided
- Customer states SGD 3.1M of net worth is from "inheritance from father's family textile business"
- Provides a letter from an Indian law firm dated 2020 stating she received assets worth INR 180,000,000 (~SGD 2.9M at 2020 rates) from her late grandfather's estate
- Remaining SGD 1.1M attributed to "business income" from Venkataraman Holdings since 2017
- No financial statements for Venkataraman Holdings provided

### 4. Adverse Media
- No results for Ms. Venkataraman personally
- Father (R. Venkataraman): Three articles from Tamil-language Indian news sources (translated summaries provided by compliance tool) referencing an ongoing state inquiry into procurement irregularities during his ministerial tenure — no charges filed as of last article date (2022)

### 5. Transaction Profile
- Expected: quarterly transfers from Indian bank account, investment in equities and real estate funds, no cash transactions

---

## Your Objectives

1. Identify what is satisfactory and what is insufficient in the evidence provided
2. List what additional documentation you would request and why
3. Assess the residual risk after a complete investigation
4. Write a brief EDD recommendation

---

## Hints

<details>
<summary>💡 Hint 1 — The RCA angle</summary>

Ms. Venkataraman is flagged as an RCA (daughter of a PEP), not a PEP herself. Does this change the level of scrutiny, or the type of scrutiny applied?

</details>

<details>
<summary>💡 Hint 2 — The inheritance documentation</summary>

A law firm letter stating value is not the same as probate documentation or estate distribution records. What specific documents would independently verify this claim?

</details>

<details>
<summary>💡 Hint 3 — The adverse media on the father</summary>

The procurement irregularities inquiry relates to the father's role as Minister of Commerce. Ms. Venkataraman's family business is in textiles. Consider whether there could be any overlap between these two facts.

</details>

---

## Model Solution

<details>
<summary>✅ Reveal Full Model Solution</summary>

### Assessment of Evidence

**Satisfactory:**
- Identity verification complete and consistent
- Singapore incorporation and directorship verified through ACRA
- RCA status (not PEP directly) confirmed — father is the PEP; Ms. Venkataraman is his daughter/RCA

**Insufficient:**

| Gap | Issue | Additional Documentation Required |
|---|---|---|
| Inheritance claim | Law firm letter alone insufficient — need independent probate evidence | Actual probate/court documents, grandfather's will, estate distribution records from Indian court |
| Business income SGD 1.1M | No financial statements provided for Venkataraman Holdings | Audited financial statements 2017–present; bank statements for the entity |
| Father's adverse media | Procurement irregularities inquiry — father's role was Commerce Minister; family business is textiles (potential overlap with procurement context) | Research what sectors were subject to the procurement inquiry; confirm no licensing/contract overlap with the textile business |
| Venkataraman Holdings revenue sources | Entity described as "investment holding and real estate advisory" — who are its clients? | Client list, source of advisory income, contracts if applicable |

### Key Risk Factors

1. **RCA status with adverse media on the PEP (father)** — the procurement inquiry, while unresolved, involves the very ministry that would oversee commercial/trade activities relevant to textiles
2. **Inheritance documentation inadequate** — SGD 2.9M of declared wealth rests on a single law firm letter; probate documents are independently verifiable and should be obtainable
3. **Business income unverified** — SGD 1.1M attributed to a Singapore holding company with no financial statements provided
4. **Potential overlap between father's ministry and family business sector** — requires explicit investigation and documentation

### Recommendation

**Conditional EDD — Do Not Approve Without:**
1. Actual Indian probate documentation (court-issued), not just a law firm letter
2. Audited financial statements for Venkataraman Holdings for all years of operation
3. A documented investigation confirming (or unable to confirm) overlap between the father's ministerial procurement inquiry and the family textile business
4. Senior management sign-off with explicit notation of the RCA/adverse media context

**Overall Risk Rating: High** (driven by RCA + unresolved adverse media on PEP father + unverified SoW components)

If probate documents and financial statements are provided and the procurement inquiry is confirmed unrelated to textile sector contracts, risk remains High but manageable with enhanced monitoring. If overlap is identified, escalate to committee review before approval.

</details>

---

## Key Learning Points

1. **A law firm's letter stating value ≠ independent verification** — always push for source documents (wills, probate, court filings)
2. **RCA investigations must examine the connection between the PEP's role and the family's business interests** — sector overlap with the PEP's area of authority is a critical additional risk dimension
3. **Absence of financial statements for an entity is a documentation gap, not a minor omission** — it leaves a significant portion of SoW unverified
