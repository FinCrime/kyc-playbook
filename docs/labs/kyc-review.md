# Lab: KYC Review

## Scenario

**Customer:** Mr. Daniel Okafor
**Nationality:** Nigerian
**Residence:** United Kingdom (London)
**Occupation:** Freelance IT Consultant
**Product Requested:** Business current account for his sole trader business "DOK Consulting"
**Expected Monthly Turnover:** £12,000–£18,000

You are a KYC Analyst reviewing the onboarding file before approval.

---

## Documents Submitted

1. **ID:** Nigerian international passport (valid, expires 2028)
2. **Proof of Address:** UK utility bill (electricity) — dated 14 months ago
3. **Business Evidence:** LinkedIn profile showing 8 years IT consulting experience; two client invoices totalling £4,200
4. **Source of Funds:** "Consulting income from UK and international clients"

---

## Screening Results

- **Sanctions:** No match
- **PEP:** No match
- **Adverse Media:** No results

---

## Your Objectives

1. Identify what is acceptable and what requires remediation
2. List specific actions required before approval
3. Assign a risk rating (Low / Medium / High) with justification
4. State what level of due diligence applies

---

## Model Solution

<details>
<summary>✅ Reveal Model Solution</summary>

### Document Assessment

| Document | Status | Issue |
|---|---|---|
| Nigerian passport | ✅ Acceptable | Valid, not expired |
| UK utility bill | ❌ Requires remediation | 14 months old — most policies require address proof within 3 months |
| LinkedIn profile | ⚠️ Supportive only | Not acceptable as standalone business evidence |
| Client invoices | ✅ Acceptable (partial) | Supports stated business activity, though only 2 invoices shown |
| Source of Funds | ⚠️ Insufficient | Verbal description only — no bank statements or contract evidence |

### Required Before Approval

1. **Current proof of address** — utility bill or bank statement dated within last 3 months
2. **Source of funds evidence** — 3–6 months of personal/business bank statements showing consulting income credits
3. **Business registration** — sole trader doesn't require Companies House registration in UK, but HMRC self-assessment registration or UTR (Unique Taxpayer Reference) confirmation would strengthen business legitimacy evidence

### Risk Rating

**Medium Risk**

**Rationale:**
- Nigerian nationality adds a geographic risk factor (Nigeria is an FATF-monitored jurisdiction and has elevated corruption perception ratings)
- Non-face-to-face onboarding (digital channel) adds slight delivery channel risk
- Freelance/self-employed income is harder to verify than salaried employment
- No adverse findings; screening is clean; stated business activity is plausible and supported by invoices

**Due Diligence Level:** Standard CDD (Medium Risk does not automatically trigger EDD; address proof and SoF evidence are standard CDD requirements)

**Not EDD-triggering because:** No PEP status, no high-risk products, business purpose is straightforward, Nigerian nationality alone does not mandate EDD under a proportionate risk-based approach (though it does elevate from Low to Medium)

</details>

---

## Key Learning Points

1. **Proof of address has a currency requirement** — most policies require documents within 3 months; 14 months is a clear fail requiring remediation before approval
2. **Nigerian nationality = Medium geographic risk factor**, not automatic EDD — proportionate, risk-based application is key
3. **LinkedIn profiles are supporting context, not primary KYC evidence**
