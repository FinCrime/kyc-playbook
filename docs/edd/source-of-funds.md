# Source of Funds (SoF)

## What Is Source of Funds?

**Source of Funds (SoF)** refers to the origin of the specific funds involved in a particular transaction or used to fund a specific account/relationship. It answers the question: **"Where did this specific money come from?"**

SoF is narrower in scope than Source of Wealth, which addresses the customer's overall accumulated net worth.

:::info SoF vs. SoW
**SoF** = Origin of funds for THIS transaction/deposit
**SoW** = Origin of overall accumulated wealth
A customer depositing $500,000 might have SoF = "sale of shares in XYZ Corp" while their SoW = "20 years as a technology executive, equity compensation, and inheritance."
:::

## When Is SoF Required?

- Standard CDD for any significant transaction
- Mandatory for all EDD cases
- Required for transactions above certain thresholds
- Required when transaction patterns deviate from expected profile
- Required for high-value cash transactions

## Acceptable SoF Evidence

| Source Type | Acceptable Evidence |
|---|---|
| Salary/Employment | Salary slips, employment contract, bank statements showing salary credits |
| Business income | Audited financial statements, tax returns, bank statements |
| Sale of asset | Sale agreement, transfer documents, bank statement showing proceeds |
| Inheritance | Will, probate documents, death certificate, estate distribution records |
| Gift | Gift deed, donor's bank statement showing the transfer, relationship verification |
| Loan | Loan agreement, lender verification, disbursement bank statement |
| Investment returns | Brokerage statements, dividend records, capital gains documentation |
| Sale of property | Sale deed, property valuation, bank statement showing proceeds |

## Investigation Approach

### Step 1: Obtain the Customer's Explanation
Ask the customer directly to explain the source of the specific funds in question.

### Step 2: Request Supporting Documentation
Require documentary evidence — never accept verbal explanation alone for EDD cases.

### Step 3: Verify Consistency
- Does the document match the claimed source?
- Is the amount consistent with the explanation (e.g., salary slip showing $5,000/month doesn't support a $500,000 deposit without additional explanation)?
- Are dates consistent (funds received before the transaction, not after)?

### Step 4: Independent Verification (Where Possible)
- Verify employer existence and legitimacy
- Cross-check property sale against public land registries
- Verify business existence and revenue plausibility

### Step 5: Document Findings
Record the evidence reviewed, verification steps taken, and conclusion reached.

## Red Flags in SoF Investigation

- Customer provides vague or inconsistent explanations
- Documentation appears altered or inconsistent in formatting
- Amount significantly exceeds what the claimed source could plausibly generate
- Customer reluctant or refuses to provide documentary evidence
- Funds traced to a third party with no clear relationship to the customer
- Funds originate from a high-risk jurisdiction or sanctioned entity
- Documentation provided is generic/templated and lacks specific transactional detail

## Case Study: SoF Investigation

**Scenario:** A customer deposits $180,000 into a newly opened account, citing "savings from business" as the source. The customer's declared occupation is a small retail shop owner.

**Investigation steps taken:**
1. Requested 12 months of business bank statements
2. Statements showed average monthly business revenue of $8,000 — savings of $180,000 over a reasonable period would require near-total income retention, which is implausible for a small retail operation
3. Requested clarification; customer then claimed partial funds were from a "friend's loan"
4. Requested loan documentation; none could be provided
5. Customer became evasive when pressed for further detail

**Conclusion:** SoF could not be adequately verified. Discrepancy between declared business income and deposit amount, combined with shifting explanations, constitutes a red flag. EDD escalated; SAR filed.
