# EDD Investigation Process

## Step-by-Step Investigation Workflow

### Step 1: Case Intake & Scoping
- Review the trigger(s) that prompted EDD
- Define the scope of investigation based on trigger type
- Pull existing customer file and KYC documentation
- Assign case priority based on risk severity

### Step 2: Document Review
- Review existing identification and CDD documents for completeness
- Identify gaps requiring additional information
- Note any documents nearing expiry

### Step 3: Enhanced Identity Verification
- Confirm identity through independent sources beyond standard CIP
- For entities: verify registration status, good standing, corporate filings
- Cross-check against multiple databases where available

### Step 4: Beneficial Ownership Mapping
For entity customers, map the complete ownership structure:
- Identify all layers of ownership down to natural persons
- Apply the [50% Rule](/docs/screening/sanctions/50-percent-rule) where relevant for sanctions purposes
- Document control relationships even where ownership is below disclosure thresholds
- Verify trust structures: settlor, trustees, beneficiaries, protector

→ [UBO Investigation](/docs/kyb/ubo/overview)

### Step 5: Source of Funds Investigation
- Request documentary evidence of the specific funds' origin
- Verify consistency between claimed source and supporting documents
- Cross-reference against known income/business activity

→ [Source of Funds Detail](/docs/edd/source-of-funds)

### Step 6: Source of Wealth Investigation (Where Applicable)
- For PEPs and high-net-worth individuals, assess overall wealth accumulation
- Request supporting documentation: tax filings, business valuations, inheritance records
- Assess plausibility relative to known career/business history

→ [Source of Wealth Detail](/docs/edd/source-of-wealth)

### Step 7: Adverse Media & OSINT Research
- Conduct comprehensive search across news archives, court records, regulatory actions
- Search in multiple languages where relevant
- Document all findings, including those assessed as not material

→ [OSINT Techniques](/docs/screening/osint/overview)

### Step 8: Sanctions/PEP Re-Screening
- Re-confirm screening results with current data
- Resolve any new hits identified

### Step 9: Risk Assessment & Documentation
- Synthesize all findings into a coherent risk narrative
- Assign or confirm risk rating
- Identify any mitigating controls

### Step 10: Decision & Approval
- Present findings to appropriate approval authority (senior management, EDD committee)
- Document the decision rationale clearly
- If declined/exited, consider SAR filing obligations

### Step 11: Enhanced Monitoring Plan
- Establish ongoing monitoring parameters specific to this customer
- Set review frequency (typically annual for EDD customers)
- Define transaction thresholds for alerts

## Investigation Time Standards (Typical Industry Benchmarks)

| Case Complexity | Typical SLA |
|---|---|
| Standard EDD (single trigger, individual) | 5–10 business days |
| Complex EDD (PEP, multi-jurisdiction) | 10–20 business days |
| Highly complex (multi-entity structures) | 20–30 business days |

## Common Pitfalls

- **Confirmation bias** — Searching only to confirm an existing assumption rather than objectively assessing all evidence
- **Insufficient documentation** — Failing to record the rationale behind decisions
- **Stopping too early** — Closing investigation once a "satisfactory" answer is found without examining red flags that emerged along the way
- **Over-reliance on customer statements** — Not seeking independent verification
