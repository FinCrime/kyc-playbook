# EDD Narrative Writing

## Why Narrative Quality Matters

An EDD narrative is the permanent written record of an investigation. It must allow a reviewer — internal QA, a regulator, or law enforcement years later — to fully understand what was found, what was concluded, and why, without needing to interview the original analyst.

## Structure of a Strong EDD Narrative

### 1. Case Background
- Customer name, account/relationship reference
- Trigger(s) that prompted EDD
- Date of investigation and analyst name

### 2. Customer Profile Summary
- Brief description of the customer (individual or entity)
- Stated occupation/business purpose
- Relationship history with the institution

### 3. Investigation Findings (By Category)
Present findings systematically:
- **Identity & Beneficial Ownership** — What was verified; any gaps
- **Source of Funds** — Documentation reviewed; conclusion on adequacy
- **Source of Wealth** (if applicable) — Documentation reviewed; plausibility assessment
- **Adverse Media/OSINT** — Summary of findings, including negative results ("no adverse media identified")
- **Sanctions/PEP Screening** — Results and any hit resolution

### 4. Risk Assessment
- Overall risk rating with rationale
- Key risk factors identified
- Mitigating factors and controls

### 5. Recommendation
- Clear recommendation: Approve / Decline / Exit / Escalate
- If approved, note conditions (enhanced monitoring, review frequency)

### 6. Approval Record
- Approver name, title, date
- Any conditions attached to approval

## Writing Principles

:::tip Best Practices
- **Be specific, not vague.** "Reviewed bank statements showing consistent salary credits of $X/month over 12 months" is far stronger than "SoF appears adequate."
- **Document negative findings too.** "No adverse media identified in searches across [sources] in [languages]" demonstrates thoroughness.
- **Avoid conclusory language without support.** Don't write "customer is low risk" without explaining why.
- **Use neutral, factual language.** Avoid speculation; stick to what was found and verified.
- **Write for a future reader who knows nothing about the case.**
:::

## Common Narrative Weaknesses

| Weakness | Example | Better Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Vague conclusions | "SoF looks fine" | "SoF verified via 6-month bank statements showing consistent $12,000/month salary credits matching declared employment at [Company]" |
| Missing negative findings | (Adverse media section omitted) | "Adverse media search conducted across [sources]; no derogatory information identified" |
| Unclear recommendation | "Customer seems okay to proceed" | "Recommend approval with annual review and enhanced transaction monitoring given PEP status" |
| No documentation of red flags addressed | (Red flag noted but not resolved in narrative) | "Initial discrepancy in declared income was resolved through [documentation]; residual risk assessed as low" |

## Sample EDD Narrative Excerpt

> **Trigger:** Customer identified as a Foreign PEP (former Deputy Minister of Trade, Country X) during onboarding screening (CLEAR database match, 94% confidence).
>
> **Beneficial Ownership:** Customer is the sole signatory and beneficial owner of the account; no corporate structure involved.
>
> **Source of Wealth:** Customer's declared net worth of approximately $2.1M is attributed to (1) government salary over 15 years of service (verified via employment history, estimated cumulative compensation $480,000) and (2) inheritance from father's estate in 2019 (probate documentation reviewed, estate valued at $1.4M, customer received 50% share per will). Remaining variance ($320,000) attributed to investment returns on inherited assets; brokerage statements reviewed confirming consistent portfolio growth.
>
> **Adverse Media:** Search conducted across English and [local language] sources via [tool]. One article identified (2017) regarding a procurement process during customer's tenure; article reports an ongoing inquiry with no findings of wrongdoing against the customer specifically. Assessed as low materiality given no adverse finding and resolution of the inquiry without charges (per follow-up search).
>
> **Recommendation:** Approve with PEP designation, annual EDD review, and enhanced transaction monitoring threshold set at $50,000.
