# AML Audit Checklist

## Purpose

This checklist supports both internal QC review and broader internal audit testing of AML program effectiveness.

## Customer Onboarding (KYC/CDD) Checklist

- [ ] Identity verified against acceptable documentation
- [ ] Risk rating assigned and documented with rationale
- [ ] Sanctions/PEP/adverse media screening completed prior to relationship establishment
- [ ] Beneficial ownership identified for entity customers
- [ ] Source of funds documented (proportionate to risk)
- [ ] Appropriate level of due diligence applied (SDD/CDD/EDD) based on risk rating

## EDD Case Checklist

- [ ] EDD trigger clearly documented
- [ ] Enhanced identity/ownership verification completed
- [ ] Source of funds and source of wealth (where applicable) documented with evidence
- [ ] Adverse media research conducted and documented (including negative results)
- [ ] Senior management approval obtained
- [ ] Risk rating appropriately assigned
- [ ] Enhanced monitoring plan established

## Transaction Monitoring Checklist

- [ ] Alert investigated within SLA
- [ ] Customer profile and transaction history reviewed
- [ ] Investigation conclusion supported by documented evidence
- [ ] SAR consideration documented regardless of filing decision

## SAR Filing Checklist

- [ ] Narrative includes who/what/when/where/why
- [ ] Specific transaction details accurately reflected
- [ ] Filed within applicable jurisdictional timeline
- [ ] Internal approval obtained prior to filing
- [ ] Confidentiality maintained (no tipping off)

## Ongoing Monitoring Checklist

- [ ] Periodic review conducted per schedule based on risk rating
- [ ] Re-screening against sanctions/PEP/adverse media performed
- [ ] Material changes in customer profile identified and addressed

## Governance Checklist

- [ ] AML policy approved by appropriate governance body and current
- [ ] Training program current and completion tracked
- [ ] Independent testing (internal audit) conducted periodically
- [ ] MLRO/Compliance Officer appointed with appropriate authority
