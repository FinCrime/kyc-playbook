# Quality Assurance (QA) and Quality Control (QC) in AML

## What Is QA/QC in AML?

**Quality Assurance (QA)** and **Quality Control (QC)** functions independently review completed AML work — alert investigations, EDD cases, SAR filings — to ensure consistency, accuracy, regulatory compliance, and adherence to internal policy. This is the function directly relevant to roles like "Compliance QA Analyst" or "QC Reviewer" — core to Vikas's actual work.

## QA vs. QC: A Useful Distinction

| | QC (Quality Control) | QA (Quality Assurance) |
|---|---|---|
| **Focus** | Individual case-level review | Process and systemic-level review |
| **Timing** | Often concurrent/shortly after case closure | Periodic, retrospective, sampling-based |
| **Output** | Pass/fail on specific cases, feedback to analyst | Trend analysis, policy recommendations, training needs |
| **Example** | "This EDD file is missing SoW documentation" | "23% of EDD files in Q2 were missing SoW documentation — indicates training gap" |

In practice, many institutions use the terms interchangeably or combine both functions within a single QA/QC team.

## What QA/QC Reviews

- Transaction monitoring alert investigations and closures
- EDD case files
- SAR filing decisions (both filed and not-filed)
- CDD/onboarding files
- Sanctions hit resolution decisions

## QA/QC Review Criteria

A QA/QC reviewer typically assesses:
1. **Completeness** — Were all required steps performed?
2. **Accuracy** — Is the information in the file factually correct?
3. **Consistency** — Was the decision consistent with similar cases and policy?
4. **Documentation quality** — Is the rationale clearly and sufficiently documented?
5. **Timeliness** — Was the case handled within SLA?
6. **Regulatory compliance** — Does the file meet applicable regulatory standards?

## Sampling Methodology

QA/QC functions typically cannot review 100% of cases, so sampling methodologies are used:
- **Risk-based sampling** — Higher proportion of high-risk cases sampled
- **Random sampling** — Statistically representative sample across the population
- **Targeted sampling** — Specific focus areas based on emerging risk or known weaknesses
- **100% review** — For certain high-risk categories (e.g., all SAR decisions, all PEP cases) some institutions mandate full review rather than sampling
