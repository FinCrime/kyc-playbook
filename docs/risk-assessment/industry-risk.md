# Industry Risk

## Overview

Industry risk assesses the inherent ML/TF vulnerability of the sector in which a customer or merchant operates.

## High-Risk Industries

| Industry | Risk Rationale |
|---|---|
| Money Service Businesses (MSBs) | Cash-intensive, often serve unbanked populations, regulatory gaps in sub-agent networks |
| Casinos / Gaming | Cash-intensive, layering vehicle |
| Precious metals & stones dealers | High value, portable, easy integration vehicle |
| Real estate | Common integration vehicle, historically weak AML oversight |
| Virtual asset service providers | Pseudonymity, cross-border speed |
| Arms dealers | Direct predicate crime linkage |
| Non-profit organizations (certain contexts) | TF misuse risk, especially in conflict-adjacent regions |
| Import/export / trading companies | TBML exposure |
| Adult entertainment | Historically associated with cash-intensive and high-risk payment patterns |
| Cannabis (where legalized) | Federal/state regulatory conflicts (US), cash-intensive |

## Lower-Risk Industries (Illustrative)

- Regulated financial institutions (subject to their own AML obligations)
- Publicly listed companies with disclosure requirements
- Government and public sector bodies
- Established retail/professional services with transparent operations
