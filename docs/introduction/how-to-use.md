# How to Use KYC Playbook

KYC Playbook is designed to be used in multiple ways depending on your needs.

## Navigation

### Sidebar
The left sidebar is organized by topic domain. Use the collapsible sections to navigate to any topic. The sidebar can be hidden by clicking the ← arrow for a distraction-free reading experience.

### Search
Use the search bar (top right, or press `/`) to instantly find any topic, term, or concept across the entire platform.

### Breadcrumbs
Every page shows breadcrumbs at the top to help you understand where you are and navigate back up the hierarchy.

### Table of Contents
Every page has a table of contents on the right sidebar, showing all headings on the current page for quick navigation.

## Content Types

### 📖 Learning Pages
Full topic guides with definitions, context, workflows, red flags, examples, case studies, checklists, and interview questions. These are the core learning material.

### 🧪 Practice Labs
Hands-on exercises simulating real-world investigations. Each lab has a scenario, objectives, evidence, hints, and a model solution.

### 🎯 Quizzes
Multiple-choice quizzes to test your knowledge after completing modules. Available under [Labs → Quizzes](/docs/labs/quizzes/aml-basics).

### 📋 Templates
Ready-to-use templates for EDD reports, SAR narratives, risk assessments, and more. Available under [Templates](/docs/templates/overview).

### 📌 Cheat Sheets
Quick-reference guides for busy analysts — AML red flags, KYC checklists, sanctions quick reference. Available under [Cheat Sheets](/docs/cheat-sheets/overview).

### 💼 Interview Prep
Role-specific interview questions with model STAR-format answers. Available under [Career → Interview](/docs/career/interview/aml-analyst).

## Features

### Dark Mode
Toggle between light and dark mode using the 🌙 icon in the top navbar.

### Edit This Page
Every page has an "Edit this page" link at the bottom — use it to contribute corrections or improvements directly via GitHub.

### Last Updated
Every page shows when it was last updated and by whom.

### Reading Time
Estimated reading time is shown on each page to help you plan your study sessions.

## Recommended Study Approach

1. **Start structured** — Follow a [Learning Path](/docs/introduction/learning-paths) rather than jumping around
2. **Take notes** — The platform is comprehensive; note what's most relevant to your role
3. **Do the labs** — Reading alone isn't enough; apply concepts through practice
4. **Take quizzes** — Test yourself after each module
5. **Revisit regularly** — Return to the cheat sheets and templates in your day-to-day work
6. **Contribute back** — When you learn something new in your work, consider contributing it
