# SAR Filing Requirements

## Filing Timelines by Jurisdiction

| Jurisdiction | Typical Timeline |
|---|---|
| United States (FinCEN) | Within 30 calendar days of initial detection (extendable to 60 days if no suspect identified) |
| United Kingdom (NCA) | "As soon as practicable" — no fixed statutory deadline, but expected promptly |
| India (FIU-IND) | Within 7 working days of confirming suspicion |
| Singapore (STRO) | "Promptly" upon forming suspicion |
| UAE (goAML) | Without delay upon forming suspicion |

:::warning Always Verify Current Requirements
SAR/STR filing timelines and procedural requirements can change. Analysts should always confirm current requirements with their compliance department and the relevant FIU's current guidance, as this table is for general educational reference only.
:::

## Required SAR Content (General Framework)

Most SAR/STR forms require:
1. **Reporting institution details**
2. **Subject information** — Customer/individual details
3. **Account/transaction details** — Specific transactions giving rise to suspicion
4. **Narrative description** — Detailed explanation of why the activity is suspicious
5. **Supporting law enforcement contact** (if applicable — prior contact with authorities about the matter)

## Continuing Activity Reports (CARs)

If suspicious activity continues after an initial SAR is filed, most jurisdictions require periodic follow-up SARs (often every 90 days in the US framework) documenting ongoing suspicious activity, rather than relying solely on the original filing.

## Recordkeeping Requirements

SAR-related records (including the SAR itself, supporting documentation, and internal decision rationale) must typically be retained for a defined period (commonly 5 years) and kept strictly confidential, accessible only to authorized personnel.
