# AML Red Flags — Quick Reference

## Transaction Red Flags

| # | Red Flag | Typology Link |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Multiple transactions just below reporting thresholds | Structuring |
| 2 | Rapid pass-through: funds in and out within 24–48 hours | Layering |
| 3 | Round-dollar amounts at unusual frequency | Structuring/Layering |
| 4 | Transactions with high-risk or sanctioned-adjacent jurisdictions | Geographic risk |
| 5 | Transactions inconsistent with customer's declared profile | Profile anomaly |
| 6 | No apparent economic purpose for the transaction | Layering |
| 7 | Multiple senders to one account, rapid onward transfer | Mule/IVTS |
| 8 | Dormant account suddenly reactivated | Account misuse |
| 9 | Trade transactions with prices far above/below market | TBML |
| 10 | Cryptocurrency linked to mixer/darknet wallet | Crypto layering |

## Customer Red Flags

| # | Red Flag |
|---|---|
| 1 | Reluctance to provide KYC documentation |
| 2 | Evasive or inconsistent answers about source of funds |
| 3 | Unexplained wealth significantly exceeding declared income |
| 4 | Requests information about reporting thresholds |
| 5 | Asks to keep transactions below specific amounts |
| 6 | Multiple related individuals opening similar accounts |
| 7 | Customer profile changes significantly without explanation |
| 8 | Third party conducting transactions on behalf of customer without disclosure |

## Entity Red Flags

| # | Red Flag |
|---|---|
| 1 | Shell company characteristics (no employees, premises, website) |
| 2 | Mass-incorporation registered address |
| 3 | Nominee directors/shareholders |
| 4 | Recently incorporated entity with no operating history |
| 5 | UBO cannot be identified after reasonable investigation |
| 6 | Ownership structure has no apparent commercial purpose |
| 7 | Multiple related entities with overlapping directors/addresses |

## Merchant-Specific Red Flags

| # | Red Flag |
|---|---|
| 1 | Statement descriptor doesn't match website/brand |
| 2 | Chargeback rate above card scheme threshold |
| 3 | Customers report receiving different products than described |
| 4 | Website non-functional or vague product descriptions |
| 5 | Missing Terms & Conditions / Refund Policy |
| 6 | Domain registered very recently relative to claimed business age |
| 7 | Transaction volume spike inconsistent with business stage |
| 8 | Multiple "storefronts" routing to same bank account |

## PEP/Screening Red Flags

| # | Red Flag |
|---|---|
| 1 | Customer is a PEP or RCA not disclosed at onboarding |
| 2 | Wealth disproportionate to public sector career |
| 3 | Family business in sector overlapping with PEP's former role/authority |
| 4 | Adverse media showing corruption/fraud allegations |
| 5 | Confirmed sanctions match (true match after hit resolution) |

## The 5 Questions to Ask on Every Case

1. **Is this transaction/activity consistent with the customer's known profile?**
2. **Can the source of funds be adequately explained and verified?**
3. **Are there any geographic, entity, or counterparty red flags?**
4. **Does the activity match a known typology?**
5. **Is there a plausible legitimate explanation — or does it require a SAR?**
