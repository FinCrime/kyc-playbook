# Adverse Media Screening

## What Is Adverse Media Screening?

**Adverse media screening** (also called negative news screening) is the process of searching public sources — news articles, court records, regulatory actions, NGO reports — for negative information about a customer that may indicate financial crime risk not captured by sanctions or PEP lists alone.

## Why Adverse Media Matters

Sanctions and PEP lists only capture individuals who have been **officially designated** or who hold **identifiable public positions**. Adverse media catches risk that exists outside these formal categories:
- Individuals under criminal investigation but not yet convicted/sanctioned
- Civil litigation alleging fraud or financial crime
- Regulatory enforcement actions
- Investigative journalism exposing corruption or financial crime
- Reputational risk issues (even absent confirmed criminal conduct)

## Categories of Adverse Media

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Financial crime** | Fraud, money laundering, embezzlement allegations |
| **Corruption** | Bribery, abuse of public office |
| **Regulatory action** | Fines, license revocations, enforcement actions |
| **Organized crime** | Links to criminal organizations |
| **Terrorism** | Links to terrorist financing or activity |
| **Sanctions evasion** | Alleged circumvention of sanctions |
| **Tax crimes** | Tax evasion, fraud allegations |
| **Other criminal conduct** | Violence, trafficking, other serious crimes |

## Severity Tiering

Not all adverse media carries equal weight. A useful tiering approach:

| Tier | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 (Severe)** | Confirmed conviction or admission of serious financial crime | Convicted of fraud |
| **Tier 2 (Significant)** | Credible allegations, ongoing investigation/litigation | Indicted for bribery, case pending |
| **Tier 3 (Moderate)** | Unsubstantiated allegations, resolved investigations with no finding | Investigated but cleared |
| **Tier 4 (Minor/Non-relevant)** | Tangential mentions, non-financial-crime negative news | Mentioned in passing in unrelated article |

## Screening Sources

- Major international news outlets
- Local/regional language news sources
- Court record databases
- Regulatory enforcement databases (SEC, FCA, etc.)
- NGO and investigative journalism reports (ICIJ, OCCRP)
- Commercial adverse media databases (often bundled with sanctions/PEP screening tools)

## Adverse Media in the EDD Process

Adverse media is a mandatory component of EDD investigation — see [EDD Investigation Process](/docs/edd/investigation-process).
