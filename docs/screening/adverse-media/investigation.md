# Adverse Media Investigation Technique

## Search Strategy

### Step 1: Construct Search Terms
- Full legal name (and known variations/transliterations)
- Name + relevant keywords: "fraud," "investigation," "charged," "sanctioned," "indicted," "money laundering," "corruption"
- Business name(s) associated with the individual
- Combine with location/nationality for disambiguation of common names

### Step 2: Search Multiple Source Types
- General search engines
- News aggregators and archives
- Court record databases (where accessible)
- Regulatory enforcement databases
- Local-language sources for non-English-speaking jurisdictions

### Step 3: Verify Source Credibility
- Prioritize established, credible news organizations
- Treat unverified social media or forum content with caution
- Cross-reference findings across multiple independent sources

### Step 4: Confirm Identity Match
- Ensure the adverse media relates to the same individual, not a namesake
- Cross-check using available identifiers (age, location, profession, photo if available)

### Step 5: Assess Materiality and Recency
- How serious is the alleged conduct?
- Is it resolved (e.g., charges dropped, acquitted) or ongoing?
- How recent is the information?

### Step 6: Document Findings
- Record search terms used, sources checked, and findings (including negative results)
- Provide a clear assessment of materiality and impact on overall risk rating

## Disambiguating Common Names

For common names, additional verification techniques:
- Cross-reference age/date of birth where available in articles
- Cross-reference profession/employer mentioned in articles against known customer information
- Cross-reference geographic location
- Look for photo matches where available (with appropriate caution about reliability)

## Handling Multi-Language Sources

For customers from non-English-speaking countries:
- Search using the local language spelling of the name
- Use translation tools to review local-language news sources
- Consider engaging local-language research resources for high-risk/high-value relationships

## Distinguishing Allegation from Conviction

| Status | Risk Treatment |
|---|---|
| Convicted | Highest severity; likely relationship decline |
| Indicted/charged, case pending | High severity; significant scrutiny, likely escalation |
| Under investigation (not charged) | Moderate-high severity depending on credibility of source/allegation |
| Investigated, no charges filed/cleared | Lower severity but document context; monitor for any subsequent developments |
| Civil litigation (non-criminal) | Context-dependent; assess nature of allegations |

## Interview Questions

1. **What search strategy would you use to research adverse media on a foreign national with a common name?**
2. **How would you handle conflicting information from different sources about the same allegation?**
3. **How do you differentiate the risk weight of an unresolved investigation versus a conviction?**
