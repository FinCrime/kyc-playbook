# UK Sanctions (OFSI)

## What Is OFSI?

The **Office of Financial Sanctions Implementation (OFSI)**, part of HM Treasury, implements and enforces financial sanctions in the UK. Following Brexit, the UK established an independent sanctions framework under the **Sanctions and Anti-Money Laundering Act 2018 (SAMLA)**.

## Key UK Sanctions Lists

- **UK Sanctions List** — The Consolidated List of all financial, trade, and other sanctions designations
- **OFSI Financial Sanctions Targets List** — Specifically asset-freeze related designations

## UK Sanctions Regimes

The UK maintains regimes covering:
- Russia (extensive measures since 2022)
- Belarus
- Syria, Iran, North Korea
- Counter-terrorism (UK and UN-derived designations)
- Global Human Rights regime (UK's "Magnitsky-style" sanctions)
- Various country-specific regimes (Myanmar, Zimbabwe, etc.)

## OFSI Reporting Obligations

Relevant firms must:
- Screen customers against the UK Sanctions List
- Report to OFSI when they identify a sanctioned person as a customer or hold their funds/assets
- Report on an annual basis when holding frozen assets (ongoing obligation while the freeze remains)

## Penalties

The Economic Crime (Transparency and Enforcement) Act 2022 strengthened OFSI's enforcement powers, introducing strict liability for certain civil monetary penalties (no longer requiring OFSI to prove knowledge or intent for some violations) and the ability to publicize breaches.
