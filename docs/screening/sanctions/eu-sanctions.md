# European Union Sanctions

## Overview

The EU implements sanctions through Council Regulations and Decisions, binding directly on all EU member states. The EU often implements UN sanctions while also maintaining autonomous sanctions regimes (notably regarding Russia following the 2022 invasion of Ukraine, and various human rights-related regimes).

## Key EU Sanctions Frameworks

- **EU Consolidated Financial Sanctions List** — Comprehensive screening list of all designated individuals/entities
- **EU Global Human Rights Sanctions Regime** — Targets human rights violators globally (EU's equivalent of "Magnitsky-style" sanctions)
- **Russia sanctions package** — Extensive sectoral and individual designations since 2014, significantly expanded from 2022

## EU AML Directives and Sanctions Compliance

The AML Directives (4AMLD–6AMLD) work alongside sanctions regulations to require:
- Customer screening against the EU Consolidated List
- Reporting of frozen assets to national competent authorities
- Enhanced due diligence for any nexus with sanctioned jurisdictions

## Post-Brexit Divergence

Following Brexit, the UK established its own independent sanctions regime (OFSI) which, while initially mirroring EU sanctions, has since diverged in some designations and implementation details — meaning EU and UK lists should be checked separately.
