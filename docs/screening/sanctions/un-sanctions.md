# United Nations Sanctions

## Overview

UN Security Council sanctions are imposed under Chapter VII of the UN Charter and are binding on all UN member states. They are typically implemented through committees overseeing specific sanctions regimes (e.g., the 1267 Committee for ISIL/Al-Qaida).

## Key UN Sanctions Regimes

| Resolution/Regime | Target |
|---|---|
| **UNSCR 1267 (and successors)** | ISIL (Da'esh) and Al-Qaida |
| **UNSCR 1718** | DPRK (North Korea) nuclear/weapons program |
| **UNSCR 2231** | Iran (nuclear-related, modified JCPOA framework) |
| **Various country-specific regimes** | Somalia, Yemen, Libya, DRC, South Sudan, Mali, CAR, and others |

## The Consolidated List

The UN maintains a **Consolidated List** of all individuals and entities subject to UN sanctions across all regimes — this is the primary screening reference.

## Implementation Variance

Although UN sanctions are technically binding, implementation occurs at the national level — meaning enforcement mechanisms and domestic legal consequences vary by member state. Most major jurisdictions (US, UK, EU) incorporate UN designations into their own domestic sanctions lists, often supplementing with additional autonomous designations.
