# OFAC Sanctions

## What Is OFAC?

The **Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC)**, part of the US Department of the Treasury, administers and enforces economic and trade sanctions against targeted foreign countries, regimes, terrorists, international narcotics traffickers, and others.

## Key OFAC Lists

| List | Description |
|---|---|
| **SDN List** (Specially Designated Nationals) | Individuals/entities whose assets are blocked; US persons prohibited from transacting with them |
| **Sectoral Sanctions Identifications (SSI) List** | Entities subject to sectoral (not comprehensive) sanctions |
| **Foreign Sanctions Evaders (FSE) List** | Persons who have violated sanctions on behalf of sanctioned countries |
| **Non-SDN Menu-Based Sanctions List** | Various other targeted designations |

## Extraterritorial Reach

OFAC sanctions apply to:
1. **US persons** — Citizens, residents, entities, and anyone physically in the US
2. **Transactions involving US dollars** — Even non-US entities transacting in USD often pass through US correspondent banks, creating OFAC nexus
3. **Secondary sanctions** — Penalize non-US persons for significant transactions with certain sanctioned parties (notably Iran, North Korea, Russia-related)

This means even institutions outside the US must consider OFAC exposure for any USD-denominated transactions.

## The 50% Rule (OFAC Specific)

OFAC's 50% Rule states that any entity owned 50% or more (in aggregate) by one or more blocked persons is itself treated as blocked, even if not separately listed on the SDN List.

→ Full detail: [50% Rule](/docs/screening/sanctions/50-percent-rule)

## General Licenses and Specific Licenses

- **General License** — Pre-authorizes certain categories of otherwise-prohibited transactions (e.g., humanitarian aid in certain contexts) without individual application
- **Specific License** — Requires individual application to OFAC for authorization of a specific transaction

## OFAC Compliance Program Expectations

OFAC has published "A Framework for OFAC Compliance Commitments" outlining five essential components:
1. Management commitment
2. Risk assessment
3. Internal controls
4. Testing and auditing
5. Training

## Penalties for Violations

OFAC penalties can be severe — civil penalties have reached hundreds of millions of dollars for major institutions, and violations can also carry criminal liability for willful violations.
