# PEP Categories

## Domestic PEPs

Senior public officials within the customer's own country. Risk level may vary based on the country's corruption risk profile and the specific role's level of influence.

## Foreign PEPs

Senior public officials of a **foreign** country. Most regulatory frameworks treat foreign PEPs as automatically requiring EDD regardless of the country's perceived risk level, since institutions may have less visibility/context into foreign political dynamics.

## International Organization PEPs

Senior officials of international bodies such as:
- United Nations and its agencies
- World Bank / IMF
- World Trade Organization
- Regional development banks
- International Olympic Committee (in some frameworks)

## Tiered Risk Approach by Role

| Role Tier | Examples | Typical Risk Level |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 (Highest) | Heads of state, ministers, senior judiciary | Highest scrutiny |
| Tier 2 | Members of parliament, senior civil servants, ambassadors | High scrutiny |
| Tier 3 | Local/municipal officials, lower-ranking civil servants | Moderate scrutiny, risk-adjusted |

Some frameworks apply a more nuanced, risk-based tiering rather than treating all PEP categories identically — particularly for lower-tier domestic PEPs in low-corruption-risk jurisdictions.
