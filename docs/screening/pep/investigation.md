# PEP Investigation Methodology

## Step-by-Step PEP Investigation

### Step 1: Confirm the Match
- Verify the screening match is genuinely the same individual (not a false positive — common name, different person)
- Compare DOB, nationality, and other identifiers

→ See [Hit Resolution](/docs/screening/sanctions/hit-resolution) for matching methodology (similar principles apply)

### Step 2: Identify the Specific Role and Tenure
- What position does/did the individual hold?
- When did they hold this position? Current or former?
- What level of influence/access did this role provide?

### Step 3: Assess Country Corruption Risk Context
- Cross-reference the relevant jurisdiction's corruption risk profile (CPI score, Basel AML Index)
- Higher corruption-risk jurisdictions warrant deeper scrutiny

### Step 4: Source of Wealth Assessment
- Mandatory for all PEP relationships
- Assess plausibility of wealth relative to known public-sector compensation

→ [Source of Wealth Detail](/docs/edd/source-of-wealth)

### Step 5: Identify RCAs
- Map family members and known close associates
- Assess whether they hold separate accounts/business interests relevant to risk assessment

### Step 6: Adverse Media Deep Dive
- Search for any allegations of corruption, abuse of office, or related criminal conduct
- Search in local language sources where possible, not just English-language media
- Document findings, including the absence of adverse findings

### Step 7: Risk Rating and Approval
- Assign appropriate risk rating (PEPs are typically automatically High Risk)
- Obtain senior management approval (mandatory under most frameworks for PEP relationships)

### Step 8: Enhanced Ongoing Monitoring
- Set lower transaction monitoring thresholds
- Schedule more frequent periodic reviews (typically annual)
- Monitor for changes in role/status

## Investigative Resources for PEP Research

- Government websites and official biographies
- Parliamentary/legislative records
- News archives (local and international)
- Court records and anti-corruption agency reports
- International organization databases (World Bank's debarred parties, etc.)
- Specialized PEP databases (World-Check, Dow Jones, LexisNexis)

## Case Study: PEP Investigation

**Scenario:** Customer is identified as the former Governor of a Central Bank in Country X (PEP, left office 3 years ago). They seek to open a substantial investment account.

**Investigation conducted:**
- Confirmed identity match through DOB and biography cross-reference
- Reviewed country corruption risk: Country X scores poorly on CPI (high corruption perception)
- Source of wealth assessment: Customer's public salary as Governor was modest; customer claims pre-existing family business wealth predates public office
- Reviewed business registration records: family business confirmed to predate the customer's tenure as Governor by 15 years, with consistent revenue history
- Adverse media: No corruption allegations found in English or local-language sources
- RCA check: Spouse holds a separate business account at a different institution; no negative findings

**Conclusion:** Wealth source is plausible and well-documented, predating public office. No adverse findings. EDD approved with PEP designation, annual review, and standard enhanced monitoring thresholds.
