# RCA: Relatives and Close Associates

## What Is an RCA?

A **Relative or Close Associate (RCA)** of a PEP is an individual who, while not themselves holding a public position, has a close personal or business relationship with a PEP that creates similar risk — namely, the potential to be used as a vehicle for hiding or layering illicitly obtained funds.

## Categories of RCAs

### Relatives (Family Members)
- Spouses and partners
- Children and their spouses/partners
- Parents
- Siblings (in stricter frameworks)

### Close Associates
- Known business partners or co-owners of entities with the PEP
- Individuals with close personal relationships publicly known or identified through investigation
- Individuals who are the beneficial owner of an entity established for the benefit of the PEP

## Why RCAs Matter

PEPs may use RCAs to:
- Hold assets in the RCA's name to obscure the PEP's true ownership/control
- Receive bribes or improper payments on the PEP's behalf
- Conduct transactions that would attract scrutiny if conducted directly by the PEP

## RCA Identification Challenges

Unlike PEPs themselves (who hold identifiable public positions), RCAs are harder to systematically identify because:
- No official public register of "close associates" exists
- Identification often depends on adverse media, business records, or customer disclosure
- Family relationships may not be immediately apparent from standard documentation

## RCA Investigation Approach

1. **Direct relatives** — Identifiable through public records, customer declaration, or biography
2. **Business associates** — Identifiable through corporate registry cross-referencing (shared directorships, co-ownership)
3. **Adverse media identification** — News coverage often identifies "close associates" implicated alongside PEPs in corruption allegations
4. **Customer declaration** — Direct questioning during onboarding ("Are you related to or closely associated with any current or former senior government official?")

## RCA Due Diligence Level

RCAs typically receive the same EDD treatment as the PEP themselves, given the equivalent risk they represent, though the specific scrutiny may focus more on their relationship to the PEP and any business dealings connected to that relationship.

1. **What is an RCA and why do they require the same scrutiny as PEPs?**
2. **What challenges exist in systematically identifying RCAs?**
