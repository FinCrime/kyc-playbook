# AML/KYC Glossary

A complete reference of terms used across the financial crime compliance field.

---

## A

**Adverse Media** — Negative information found in public sources (news, court records, regulatory actions) that may indicate financial crime risk. Also called negative news screening.

**AML** — Anti-Money Laundering. The set of laws, regulations, and procedures designed to prevent criminals from disguising illegally obtained funds as legitimate income.

**AML Program** — An institution's documented framework for preventing, detecting, and reporting money laundering and other financial crimes, typically comprising written policies, a designated AML officer, training, and independent testing.

**AUSTRAC** — Australian Transaction Reports and Analysis Centre. Australia's FIU and AML/CTF regulator.

---

## B

**Bank Secrecy Act (BSA)** — The primary US AML legislation, requiring financial institutions to assist government agencies in detecting and preventing money laundering.

**Beneficial Owner / UBO** — The natural person(s) who ultimately own or control a legal entity or arrangement, even if that ownership is exercised through intermediate entities or nominees.

**Black List (FATF)** — FATF's "High-Risk Jurisdictions subject to a Call for Action" — countries with severe AML/CFT deficiencies. See also: Grey List.

---

## C

**CAMS** — Certified Anti-Money Laundering Specialist. The ACAMS-administered professional certification widely recognized in the AML field.

**CAR** — Continuing Activity Report. A follow-up SAR filed when suspicious activity continues beyond an initial filing (typically every 90 days in the US framework).

**CDD** — Customer Due Diligence. The process of verifying identity, understanding business purpose, assessing risk, and establishing expected transaction patterns for a customer.

**CFT** — Countering the Financing of Terrorism. The framework addressing financial flows to terrorist organizations, distinct from AML (which addresses criminal proceeds).

**CIP** — Customer Identification Programme. The regulatory requirement to verify customer identity before establishing a business relationship.

**CTR** — Currency Transaction Report. A mandatory report filed for cash transactions exceeding the applicable threshold (e.g., USD 10,000 in the US).

---

## D

**Designated Non-Financial Businesses and Professions (DNFBPs)** — Non-bank entities subject to AML obligations under FATF standards, including lawyers, accountants, real estate agents, and dealers in precious metals and stones.

---

## E

**EDD** — Enhanced Due Diligence. A deeper level of customer due diligence applied to higher-risk customers, requiring more extensive investigation of identity, source of funds/wealth, and ongoing monitoring.

**EWRA** — Enterprise-Wide Risk Assessment. A holistic assessment of an institution's overall exposure to ML/TF risk across its entire business.

---

## F

**FATF** — Financial Action Task Force. The intergovernmental body that sets global AML/CFT standards through its 40 Recommendations.

**FinCEN** — Financial Crimes Enforcement Network. The US Treasury bureau that administers the BSA, maintains AML/CTR/SAR reporting infrastructure, and issues guidance.

**FIU** — Financial Intelligence Unit. A national body that receives, analyzes, and disseminates SAR/STR reports to law enforcement (e.g., NCA/UKFIU in the UK, FinCEN in the US, FIU-IND in India).

**Front Company** — A legitimate-appearing business used to commingle criminal proceeds with genuine revenues, typically in cash-intensive industries.

**Fuzzy Matching** — A screening technique that identifies approximate name matches (accounting for variations, transliterations, and aliases) rather than requiring exact character-for-character matches.

---

## G

**goAML** — A software platform developed by UNODC used by many national FIUs for electronic SAR/STR submission.

**Grey List (FATF)** — FATF's "Jurisdictions under Increased Monitoring" — countries actively working with FATF to address AML/CFT deficiencies. See also: Black List.

---

## H

**Hawala** — An informal value transfer system based on trust networks, where value is transferred without physical movement of currency. A recognized ML/TF typology when misused.

---

## I

**Integration** — The third stage of money laundering, where laundered funds re-enter the legitimate economy as apparently clean assets.

**IVTS** — Informal Value Transfer System. A collective term for systems like hawala that transfer value without physical currency movement.

---

## K

**KYB** — Know Your Business. The entity-level equivalent of KYC, involving verification of a business's legitimacy, ownership structure, and financial crime risk.

**KYC** — Know Your Customer. The process of verifying customer identity, understanding business purpose, and assessing AML risk.

---

## L

**Layering** — The second stage of money laundering, involving complex transactions designed to obscure the origin of criminal proceeds.

---

## M

**MCC** — Merchant Category Code. A four-digit code classifying a merchant's business type, used by card schemes to categorize transactions and assess risk.

**MLRO** — Money Laundering Reporting Officer. The senior individual responsible for AML compliance at a UK-regulated firm, with specific SAR filing obligations.

**MSB** — Money Service Business. A non-bank financial institution providing services such as currency exchange, money transmission, or prepaid access.

---

## N

**NCA** — National Crime Agency. The UK's lead agency for serious and organized crime, which also administers the UK FIU (UKFIU) for SAR receipt and analysis.

**Nominee** — A person who acts in a formal capacity (director, shareholder) on behalf of the true owner/controller without disclosing the underlying principal.

---

## O

**OFAC** — Office of Foreign Assets Control. The US Treasury body that administers and enforces economic and trade sanctions.

**OFSI** — Office of Financial Sanctions Implementation. The UK Treasury body that implements and enforces UK financial sanctions.

**Ongoing Monitoring** — The continuous process of monitoring transactions for consistency with customer profiles and periodically reviewing/updating KYC information.

---

## P

**PayFac** — Payment Facilitator. A PSP that onboards sub-merchants under its own master merchant account, taking on responsibility for their KYB/MDD and ongoing monitoring.

**PEP** — Politically Exposed Person. An individual who holds or has held a prominent public position, carrying elevated risk of involvement in bribery or corruption.

**Placement** — The first stage of money laundering, introducing criminal proceeds into the financial system.

**Predicate Offense** — The underlying criminal activity generating the proceeds that are subsequently laundered.

**PSC Register** — Persons with Significant Control Register. UK's publicly accessible register of individuals with significant ownership or control over UK-registered companies.

**PSP** — Payment Service Provider. A company providing payment processing services to merchants, which may combine gateway and acquiring functions.

---

## Q

**QA** — Quality Assurance. The function providing systemic oversight of AML program effectiveness, identifying trends and process improvement needs.

**QC** — Quality Control. The function reviewing individual cases against quality standards, providing case-level feedback and pass/fail decisions.

---

## R

**RBA** — Risk-Based Approach. The principle of calibrating AML controls proportionately to assessed ML/TF risk — more scrutiny for higher-risk customers/activities.

**RCA** — Relative or Close Associate (of a PEP). Family members and known close associates who may present similar corruption-related financial crime risk to the PEP themselves.

**Risk Rating** — A classification (typically Low/Medium/High) assigned to a customer based on assessed ML/TF risk factors, determining the level of due diligence and monitoring applied.

---

## S

**SAR** — Suspicious Activity Report. A report filed with a national FIU when there are reasonable grounds to suspect criminal activity connected to money laundering or related crimes. Also called STR in many jurisdictions.

**SDD** — Simplified Due Diligence. A reduced (but not eliminated) level of CDD applied to lower-risk customers.

**SDN List** — Specially Designated Nationals List. OFAC's primary list of blocked individuals and entities whose assets must be frozen by US persons.

**Shell Company** — A legal entity with no genuine business operations, often used to obscure beneficial ownership or facilitate layering.

**SoF** — Source of Funds. The origin of specific funds used in a transaction or to fund an account.

**SoW** — Source of Wealth. The origin of a customer's overall accumulated net worth over their lifetime.

**STR** — Suspicious Transaction Report. The equivalent of a SAR in many non-US jurisdictions (India, Singapore, UAE, etc.).

**Structuring** — The deliberate breaking up of transactions to avoid reporting thresholds; also called smurfing. A standalone offense in most jurisdictions regardless of the source of funds.

---

## T

**TBML** — Trade-Based Money Laundering. The use of international trade transactions (over/under-invoicing, misrepresentation of goods) to transfer value across borders and launder proceeds.

**Tipping Off** — Informing a customer or third party that a SAR has been or may be filed, which is a criminal offense in most jurisdictions as it may compromise criminal investigations.

**Transaction Laundering** — The processing of payments for an undisclosed, prohibited merchant through a legitimate merchant's payment account.

**Transaction Monitoring** — The ongoing automated and manual review of customer transactions to identify suspicious patterns.

**Trust** — A legal arrangement where a trustee holds and manages assets for the benefit of beneficiaries according to the terms established by the settlor.

---

## U

**UBO** — Ultimate Beneficial Owner. The natural person(s) who ultimately own or control a legal entity, after tracing through all intermediate ownership layers.

**UNSCR** — United Nations Security Council Resolution. Legally binding resolutions (under Chapter VII of the UN Charter) authorizing sanctions regimes binding on all UN member states.

---

## V

**VASP** — Virtual Asset Service Provider. An entity that conducts virtual asset exchange, transfer, or custody services, subject to AML/CFT obligations under FATF Recommendation 15.

---

## W

**World-Check** — A commercial KYC/screening database (Refinitiv/LSEG) aggregating sanctions lists, PEP data, and adverse media across global jurisdictions.
