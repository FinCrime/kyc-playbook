# CAMS — Certified Anti-Money Laundering Specialist

## What Is CAMS?

**CAMS (Certified Anti-Money Laundering Specialist)** is the most widely recognized professional certification in the AML/financial crime compliance field, administered by **ACAMS (Association of Certified Anti-Money Laundering Specialists)**. It is considered a strong credential for career advancement across banking, fintech, and consulting roles globally.

## CAMS Exam Domains (Typical Structure)

The CAMS exam covers five core domains. Always verify the exact current domain weighting directly from ACAMS, as exam content specifications are periodically updated:

1. **Risks and Methods of Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing**
2. **Compliance Standards for AML/CFT/Sanctions Programs Globally**
3. **AML/CFT/Sanctions Compliance Program**
4. **Conducting and Supporting the Investigations Process**
5. **Additional domain content** (varies by current exam version — confirm with ACAMS' current exam content outline)

## Suggested 84-Day Study Plan Framework

This is an illustrative structure; adjust based on your own starting knowledge level and available study time.

### Phase 1: Foundations (Days 1–21)
- Money laundering stages, predicate crimes, typologies
- FATF 40 Recommendations overview
- Major regulatory frameworks (BSA, MLR 2017, EU AMLDs, PMLA)

### Phase 2: Compliance Program Components (Days 22–42)
- CDD/EDD/KYC frameworks
- Risk assessment methodology
- Sanctions and PEP screening
- Three Lines of Defence and governance structures

### Phase 3: Investigations and Reporting (Days 43–63)
- Transaction monitoring methodology
- SAR/STR filing requirements across jurisdictions
- Case study practice — applying typology knowledge to scenarios

### Phase 4: Review and Practice Exams (Days 64–84)
- Full-length practice exams under timed conditions
- Targeted review of weak domains identified through practice testing
- Flashcard review of key definitions, thresholds, and regulatory citations

## Free Study Resources

- **FATF Publications** — Recommendations, guidance papers, mutual evaluation reports (fatf-gafi.org)
- **FinCEN Advisories** — Real-world typology guidance (fincen.gov)
- **ACAMS Today articles** — Many freely accessible
- **National FIU annual reports** — Often contain useful typology and statistics data
- **This platform** — Comprehensive coverage of all core AML/KYC/EDD concepts

## Exam Format Considerations

CAMS exam format details (number of questions, time allowed, passing score, question types) are set by ACAMS and may change — **always verify current exam specifications directly from ACAMS' official website** before finalizing your study plan, as third-party sources (including this guide) may not reflect the most current exam structure.

## Why CAMS Matters for Career Progression

CAMS certification is frequently:
- Listed as a preferred or required qualification in job postings for AML Analyst, EDD Specialist, and Compliance Manager roles
- Associated with salary premiums in industry compensation surveys
- A credential recognized internationally, supporting career moves across jurisdictions (relevant for cross-border career goals)

## Interview Questions Related to CAMS Knowledge

1. **What are the five pillars of an AML compliance program?**
2. **What is the FATF Risk-Based Approach and why is it foundational?**
3. **Explain the three stages of money laundering with examples.**
