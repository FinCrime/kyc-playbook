# CFCS — Certified Financial Crime Specialist

## What Is CFCS?

**CFCS (Certified Financial Crime Specialist)** is a certification administered by the **Association of Certified Financial Crime Specialists (ACFCS)**. It covers a broader scope than AML alone, encompassing fraud, cybercrime, and other financial crime disciplines alongside traditional money laundering topics.

## CFCS vs. CAMS

| | CAMS | CFCS |
|---|---|---|
| **Administering Body** | ACAMS | ACFCS |
| **Primary Focus** | AML/CFT specifically | Broader financial crime (AML + fraud + cyber + sanctions) |
| **Industry Recognition** | Most widely recognized for pure AML roles | Valued for broader financial crime/fraud-adjacent roles |

## Typical Content Areas

- Money laundering and terrorist financing
- Fraud schemes and detection
- Cybercrime and financial crime technology
- Sanctions and trade-based financial crime
- Investigation techniques

:::tip Verify Current Details
Certification content, format, and requirements change periodically. Always check ACFCS's official website for the current exam blueprint before beginning study preparation.
:::

## Who Should Consider CFCS

CFCS may be particularly relevant for professionals whose roles span beyond pure AML into fraud investigation, cyber-enabled financial crime, or broader financial crime risk management — a complementary credential to CAMS for those seeking broader financial crime expertise.
