# Compliance QA / QC Analyst Interview Questions

## Technical Knowledge Questions

**1. What is the difference between QA and QC in an AML context?**

*Model Answer:* QC typically refers to case-level review of individual investigations against defined standards, providing pass/fail feedback to the analyst. QA refers to broader, often trend-based review of the overall process — identifying systemic patterns, training needs, and policy gaps across a population of cases, not just individual case outcomes.

**2. Walk through your process for reviewing an EDD case file.**

*Model Answer:* I'd check completeness against the institution's EDD checklist (identity/ownership verification, SoF/SoW documentation, adverse media research, sanctions re-screening), assess whether the documented evidence logically supports the conclusion reached, verify required approvals were obtained, and check timeliness against SLA. I'd categorize any deficiency by severity and provide specific, actionable feedback.

**3. How would you classify a deficiency where an analyst closed an alert without documenting their research, but the underlying decision was likely correct?**

*Model Answer:* This would be a documentation deficiency — likely "Major" rather than "Critical" since the substantive decision may be correct, but the lack of documented rationale is itself a significant gap that would concern a regulator reviewing the file. Documentation deficiencies shouldn't be dismissed just because the outcome seems right — the file must independently demonstrate sound reasoning.

## Behavioral Questions

**4. Describe a time you identified a systemic issue through QA sampling, not just an individual case error.**

*Approach:* Describe a pattern you noticed across multiple reviewed cases (e.g., consistent gaps in SoW documentation for a particular product type), how you escalated it, and what came of the escalation (training update, policy clarification, etc.).

**5. How do you deliver critical feedback to an analyst without damaging the working relationship?**

*Approach:* Discuss specific, evidence-based feedback delivery, balancing constructive criticism with recognition of good practice, and framing feedback around improving outcomes rather than personal criticism.

**6. Tell me about a time you disagreed with a colleague's risk assessment and how you resolved it.**

*Approach:* Describe a specific scenario, your reasoning for disagreement, how you raised it professionally, and the resolution process (escalation, discussion, compromise, or accepting the other view with new information).

## Scenario-Based Questions

**7. You're reviewing 50 EDD cases and find that 12 are missing documented adverse media search results, though all 12 show "no adverse media found" as the conclusion. What's your assessment and recommendation?**

*Model Answer:* This is a meaningful documentation gap rather than necessarily an incorrect substantive conclusion — but a 24% rate across the sample suggests a systemic training/process issue, not isolated error. I would flag this as a QA finding (not just 12 individual QC failures), recommend a process reminder/training refresher to the team, and potentially recommend a template update to mandate explicit recording of search terms/sources used for adverse media checks going forward.

**8. How would you handle finding evidence that an analyst backdated documentation in a case file?**

*Model Answer:* This is a serious integrity issue beyond a standard quality deficiency — it would need immediate escalation to management/compliance leadership rather than standard QC feedback, given the potential regulatory and ethical implications of falsified records.
