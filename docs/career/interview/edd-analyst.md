# EDD Analyst Interview Questions

## Technical Knowledge Questions

**1. Walk through your complete EDD investigation process from trigger to decision.**

*Model Answer:* See [EDD Investigation Process](/docs/edd/investigation-process) for the full framework: case scoping, document review, enhanced identity/ownership verification, source of funds and source of wealth investigation, adverse media/OSINT research, sanctions re-screening, risk assessment synthesis, and senior approval.

**2. What's the difference between Source of Funds and Source of Wealth?**

*Model Answer:* Source of Funds addresses the origin of the specific money used in a particular transaction. Source of Wealth addresses how the customer accumulated their overall net worth over time. SoW is required for PEPs and high-net-worth individuals; SoF is required more broadly for significant transactions.

**3. How do you assess the plausibility of a source of wealth claim?**

*Model Answer:* I cross-reference the claimed wealth accumulation story against verifiable facts — career history, business records, documented transactions — and assess whether the magnitude and timing of claimed wealth is consistent with what those facts would plausibly generate. Significant unexplained gaps between claimed wealth and verifiable income sources are red flags requiring further investigation or decline.

## Behavioral Questions

**4. Describe the most complex EDD case you've handled.**

*Approach:* Choose a case (anonymized) involving multiple risk factors — e.g., multi-jurisdiction ownership structure plus PEP status — and walk through your systematic approach to unpacking the complexity, what you found, and your final recommendation.

**5. Tell me about a time you recommended declining a relationship despite commercial pressure to approve it.**

*Approach:* Describe a case where business pressure existed to approve a profitable relationship, but your investigation surfaced unresolvable red flags, and how you documented and communicated your recommendation despite that pressure.

## Scenario-Based Questions

**6. A PEP customer's declared net worth significantly exceeds what their public salary history would generate, but they claim "family inheritance" with no documentation. How do you proceed?**

*Model Answer:* An unsubstantiated claim of this magnitude is insufficient for EDD approval. I would request specific documentary evidence (will, probate records, estate distribution records) and, absent adequate documentation, would not accept the explanation at face value. If documentation cannot be provided after reasonable opportunity, I would recommend escalation or decline given the unresolved discrepancy between declared and verifiable wealth.

**7. You discover during an EDD investigation that the customer's beneficial ownership chain terminates in a jurisdiction with no public UBO registry. What do you do?**

*Model Answer:* I would request direct documentary evidence from the customer (trust deeds, notarized ownership declarations, registered agent confirmations) since public registry verification isn't available. I would also apply additional scrutiny given the jurisdiction's opacity — assessing whether the structure has any other red flags warranting elevated risk treatment, and clearly document the limitation in available independent verification in my case file.
