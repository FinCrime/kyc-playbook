# KYC Analyst Interview Questions

## Technical Knowledge Questions

**1. Walk me through your KYC onboarding process for a new individual customer.**

*Model Answer:* I'd begin with identity verification (government ID, address proof), then conduct sanctions/PEP/adverse media screening, assess the business purpose and expected transaction profile, assign a risk rating based on relevant factors (geography, occupation, product), and apply the appropriate level of due diligence (SDD/CDD/EDD) based on that rating.

**2. What's the difference between CDD and EDD?**

*Model Answer:* CDD is the standard due diligence applied to most customers — identity verification, understanding business purpose, basic source of funds confirmation. EDD is a deeper level applied to higher-risk customers (PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, complex structures), requiring more extensive documentation, source of wealth assessment, and senior management approval.

**3. What documents would you require for a corporate customer?**

*Model Answer:* Certificate of incorporation, Memorandum & Articles of Association, register of directors and shareholders, UBO declaration with supporting evidence, proof of business address, and where relevant, business licenses appropriate to the regulated activity.

**4. How do you handle a customer who is reluctant to provide documentation?**

*Model Answer:* I'd clearly explain the regulatory basis for the request and the consequence of not providing it (delayed/declined onboarding). If reluctance persists without reasonable explanation, this itself becomes a risk factor warranting escalation — legitimate customers generally understand and comply with standard KYC requirements.

## Behavioral Questions

**5. Describe a time you identified an inconsistency in a customer's KYC documentation.**

*Approach:* Describe a specific example of cross-checking documents (e.g., address mismatch between ID and utility bill, name spelling inconsistency) and how you resolved or escalated it.

**6. How do you stay current with KYC regulatory changes?**

*Approach:* Discuss specific resources you use (regulatory bulletins, industry training, internal policy updates) and how you've applied a regulatory change to your actual work.

## Scenario-Based Questions

**7. A business customer's UBO declaration shows ownership split among 5 individuals, none exceeding 20% individually. How do you proceed?**

*Model Answer:* Since no individual meets the standard 25% UBO ownership threshold, I would assess control-based criteria instead — does any individual exercise significant control through board representation, voting agreements, or other means? If genuinely no one meets ownership or control thresholds, I'd identify the senior managing official as a measure of last resort, while documenting the full ownership picture for risk assessment purposes.

**8. How would you risk-rate a new individual customer who is a non-resident with no prior relationship history with your institution?**

*Model Answer:* Non-resident status without prior relationship history typically elevates risk somewhat due to limited verification context and potentially higher geographic risk depending on the specific jurisdiction. I'd assess: country risk of residence, product/service requested, source of funds clarity, and apply correspondingly enhanced verification before assigning a final risk rating — likely starting from a baseline assumption of at least medium risk pending full assessment.
