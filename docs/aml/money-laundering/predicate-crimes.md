# Predicate Crimes

## What Are Predicate Crimes?

A **predicate crime** (or **predicate offense**) is the underlying criminal activity that generates the illicit proceeds that are subsequently laundered. Money laundering cannot exist without a predicate offense — the funds must be criminal in origin.

Under FATF Recommendation 3, countries are required to criminalize money laundering for a designated range of predicate offenses that are listed in FATF's Glossary.

|---|---|---|
| 1 | Participation in organized crime | Racketeering, criminal enterprise |

| 3 | Trafficking in human beings and migrant smuggling | Sex trafficking, labor exploitation |

## High-Volume Predicate Offenses in Practice

### Drug Trafficking
The largest source of criminal proceeds globally. Drug trafficking organizations (DTOs) generate billions in cash that must be laundered. Common patterns:
- Bulk cash smuggling across borders
- Use of money service businesses
- Real estate purchases in resort/tourist areas
- Restaurant and hospitality sector commingling

### Fraud (Including BEC and Cyber-Enabled Fraud)
Business Email Compromise (BEC), romance fraud, investment fraud, and advance fee fraud generate significant proceeds. Common patterns:
- Funds received in mule accounts and rapidly forwarded
- Wire transfers to cryptocurrency exchanges
- Use of money mule networks

### Corruption and Bribery
PEPs and associated individuals launder bribery proceeds through:
- Offshore accounts in secrecy jurisdictions
- Real estate in foreign jurisdictions
- Shell company networks
- Front businesses

### Tax Evasion
Proceeds of tax evasion are predicate offenses in most jurisdictions. Round-tripping through offshore entities is particularly common.

## Why Predicate Crimes Matter for AML Analysts

Understanding predicate crimes helps analysts:

1. **Contextualize suspicious patterns** — A large cash business is more suspicious in a drug-trafficking context than a restaurant context
2. **Identify typologies** — Each predicate offense has associated money laundering typologies
3. **Write better SAR narratives** — A strong SAR specifies the suspected predicate offense where identifiable
4. **Apply risk assessments** — Certain industries or customer types are associated with specific predicate offenses

## Interview Questions

1. **What is a predicate offense?**
   *A predicate offense is the underlying criminal activity that generates the proceeds to be laundered. Without a predicate offense, there are no criminal proceeds and no money laundering.*

2. **Give an example of a predicate crime and describe how proceeds might be laundered.**
   *Drug trafficking generates cash. The trafficker uses smurfs to structure deposits below the $10,000 CTR threshold (placement), transfers funds through shell companies in multiple jurisdictions (layering), and ultimately purchases real estate (integration).*
