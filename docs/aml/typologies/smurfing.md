# Smurfing (Structuring)

## What Is Smurfing?

**Smurfing** (also called **structuring**) is a money laundering technique that involves breaking up large amounts of cash into smaller deposits, purchases, or transactions to avoid triggering mandatory reporting thresholds.

The term comes from the small blue cartoon characters — "smurfs" — reflecting how multiple small actors work together to move large amounts without attracting attention.

## How It Works

In the United States, banks are required to file a **Currency Transaction Report (CTR)** for any cash transaction over **$10,000**. Smurfing deliberately keeps transactions below this threshold:

**Example:** A drug trafficker has $90,000 in cash. Instead of depositing it in one transaction, they:
- Recruit 10 individuals ("smurfs")
- Each smurf deposits $8,500–$9,500 at different bank branches
- No single transaction exceeds $10,000
- No CTR is filed
- The total $90,000 enters the financial system

## Key Legal Points

- **Structuring is itself a federal crime** under 31 U.S.C. § 5324 (USA), even if the underlying funds are from a legitimate source
- The intent to evade reporting is what makes it illegal — not the source of the funds
- Financial institutions must file a SAR if they suspect structuring, even if no CTR is triggered

## Detection Techniques

**Transaction pattern analysis:**
- Multiple cash deposits just below the reporting threshold in a short period
- Deposits across multiple branches or ATMs on the same day
- Multiple individuals making deposits to the same account
- Round amounts slightly below thresholds (e.g., $9,900, $9,800, $9,750)

**Account-level analysis:**
- Pattern of deposits that, in aggregate, exceed thresholds even if individual deposits don't
- Sudden change in transaction behavior (new frequent cash deposits)
- Deposits inconsistent with customer's occupation or stated income

## Red Flags

- Multiple deposits of $9,000–$9,999 within 30 days
- Multiple individuals depositing to one account
- Customer requests that transactions not be reported
- Customer mentions awareness of reporting thresholds
- Deposits at multiple branches of the same bank on the same day
