# Trade-Based Money Laundering (TBML)

## What Is TBML?

Trade-Based Money Laundering (TBML) is the process of disguising the proceeds of crime and moving value through the use of legitimate trade transactions. It is one of the most significant and underdetected methods of money laundering, used to launder hundreds of billions annually.

FATF defines TBML as "the process of disguising the proceeds of crime and moving value through the use of trade transactions in an attempt to legitimise their illegal origin."

## Core Mechanisms

### 1. Over-Invoicing
Goods or services are invoiced at a value higher than their actual market price. The importing country pays the inflated price, and the excess value represents a transfer from importer to exporter.

**Example:** Importer in Country A pays $2M for widgets actually worth $1.2M. The $800K excess moves value from Country A to Country B's exporter — a criminal transfer disguised as trade.

### 2. Under-Invoicing
Goods are invoiced below their actual value. The exporter receives less foreign exchange than the goods' true value, effectively retaining value offshore.

### 3. Multiple Invoicing
The same shipment of goods is invoiced multiple times to multiple parties, allowing multiple payments for the same goods.

### 4. Falsely Described Goods
Goods are described as a lower-value category to reduce duties, or described as a different product entirely.

### 5. Over/Under-Shipment
More or fewer goods are shipped than invoiced — creating a value discrepancy between the paper trail and physical reality.

## Red Flags

| Red Flag | Category |
|---|---|
| Invoice price significantly above/below market price | Mis-invoicing |
| Multiple payments for one shipment | Multiple invoicing |
| Payments from unrelated third parties | Third-party payments |
| Transactions with high-risk jurisdictions | Geographic risk |
| No verifiable counterparty business | Entity risk |
| Payments inconsistent with stated trade volumes | Pattern analysis |
| Round-dollar trade transactions | Transaction pattern |
| Complex routing of payments | Layering |

## Detection Sources

- **PIERS / Global Trade Atlas** — Trade statistics for benchmarking
- **World Customs Organization (WCO)** — Customs intelligence
- **IMF Direction of Trade Statistics** — Bilateral trade flows
- **Company accounts** — Inconsistencies between declared trade and financial statements
