# Terrorist Financing Red Flags

## Transaction Red Flags

- Transactions to/from sanctioned jurisdictions or entities
- Small, frequent transfers to conflict zones with no commercial purpose
- Receipt of funds from NPOs in high-risk regions followed by immediate onward transfer
- Multiple small deposits from individuals in different countries, consolidated and forwarded
- Cash transactions with no clear business purpose in high-risk regions
- Cryptocurrency transactions linked to sanctioned wallets

## Customer Red Flags

- Customer has no verifiable employment but makes regular international transfers
- Customer expresses extremist views or appears associated with extremist networks
- Customer is a national of or has close ties to a sanctioned country
- Customer profile inconsistent with transaction patterns
- Customer recently traveled to conflict zones

## Business/Entity Red Flags

- NPO registered in or operating in conflict areas
- Vague programmatic descriptions for NPO activities
- NPO with no verifiable beneficiaries
- Transactions matching known TF typologies (hawala networks, cryptocurrency donation addresses publicized on extremist forums)

## Screening Red Flags

- Customer name closely matches sanctioned individual or terrorist designation
- Customer associates with known PEPs who have TF-related adverse media
- Transactions reference known terrorist groups or designated entities

## Interview Questions

1. **How do you differentiate a TF suspicion from a general AML suspicion in a SAR narrative?**
2. **What makes NPOs particularly vulnerable to TF misuse?**
3. **If a customer sends small regular payments to a country with active terrorist operations, what are your next steps?**
