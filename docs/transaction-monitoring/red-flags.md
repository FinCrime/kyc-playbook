# Transaction Monitoring Red Flags

## Account-Level Red Flags

- Sudden, unexplained increase in transaction volume or frequency
- Account used as a "pass-through" with minimal retention of funds
- Multiple related accounts conducting similar/coordinated transactions
- Dormant account suddenly reactivated with high-value transactions

## Transaction-Level Red Flags

- Round-dollar transactions occurring with unusual frequency
- Transactions structured just below reporting/alert thresholds
- Transactions inconsistent with the stated business purpose
- High-value transactions with no clear economic rationale
- Multiple small transactions aggregating to a large total in a short period

## Counterparty Red Flags

- Transactions with counterparties in high-risk or sanctioned-adjacent jurisdictions
- Repeated transactions with previously flagged/suspicious accounts
- Counterparties that cannot be identified or verified

## Behavioral Red Flags

- Customer requests to keep transactions below reporting thresholds
- Customer is evasive or provides inconsistent explanations when questioned
- Customer requests unusual urgency in processing transactions
- Customer uses multiple identities or representatives for related transactions

## Industry-Specific Red Flag Examples

| Context | Red Flag |
|---|---|
| Payment gateway/merchant | Chargeback spike coinciding with website content changes |
| Trade finance | Invoice value significantly inconsistent with market price for goods |
| Correspondent banking | Nested correspondent relationships obscuring the originating institution |
| Crypto exchange | Funds traced to known mixer/darknet market addresses |
