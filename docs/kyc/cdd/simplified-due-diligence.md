# Simplified Due Diligence (SDD)

## What Is SDD?

**Simplified Due Diligence** is a reduced level of CDD applied to customers assessed as presenting low risk of money laundering or terrorist financing. SDD reduces — but never eliminates — the due diligence requirements.

:::warning Important
SDD is not "no due diligence." Identity verification is still required. SDD only reduces the depth of ongoing monitoring and additional information gathering — it never permits skipping basic identification.
:::

## When SDD May Apply

Common categories eligible for SDD (varies by jurisdiction):

- Government departments and public bodies
- Publicly listed companies subject to disclosure requirements
- Regulated financial institutions in equivalent jurisdictions
- Low-value, low-risk products (e.g., certain pension products, basic savings accounts below thresholds)
- Customers in low-risk jurisdictions with straightforward, low-risk products

## SDD Requirements (Reduced, Not Eliminated)

- Basic identity verification still required
- Reduced frequency of ongoing monitoring
- Reduced documentation requirements for source of funds
- Lower frequency periodic reviews

## Risks of Over-Applying SDD

Regulators have penalized institutions for applying SDD too broadly. Common failures:
- Applying SDD based solely on customer type without considering other risk factors
- Failing to recognize when a "low-risk" customer's behavior changes (requiring re-rating)
- Not having a documented rationale for SDD classification
