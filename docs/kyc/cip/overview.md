# Customer Identification Programme (CIP)

## What Is CIP?

The **Customer Identification Programme (CIP)** is the foundational process of verifying a customer's identity before establishing a business relationship. It is the first line of defense in the KYC framework and a mandatory regulatory requirement in virtually every jurisdiction with AML laws.

:::info Regulatory Basis
- **USA PATRIOT Act Section 326** — Requires financial institutions to implement a written CIP
- **MLR 2017 Regulation 28** (UK) — Customer due diligence measures
- **FATF Recommendation 10** — Customer due diligence
:::

## The Four Pillars of CIP (US Framework)

1. **Collect identifying information** — Name, date of birth, address, identification number
2. **Verify identity** — Through documentary or non-documentary methods
3. **Maintain records** — Retain CIP records for required periods (typically 5 years)
4. **Check government lists** — Screen against OFAC and other sanctions/watchlists

## Identity Verification Methods

### Documentary Verification
- Government-issued photo ID (passport, driver's license, national ID card)
- Cross-check document features: photo, security features, expiry date
- Document authenticity verification (holograms, MRZ codes, watermarks)

### Non-Documentary Verification
- Database checks (credit bureaus, government databases)
- Comparison of information provided against independent sources
- Contacting the customer directly

### Biometric Verification
Modern KYC increasingly relies on biometric methods:
- Facial recognition matching (selfie vs. ID photo)
- Liveness detection (proving the person is physically present, not a photo/video)
- Fingerprint or other biometric markers (where applicable)

→ [Biometric Verification](/docs/kyc/cip/biometric-verification)

## CIP for Different Customer Types

| Customer Type | Key Identifiers |
|---|---|
| Individual | Name, DOB, address, government ID number |
| Sole proprietor | Individual identifiers + business name/registration |
| Corporation/LLC | Legal name, registration number, business address, beneficial owners |
| Trust | Trust name, trustee identity, settlor, beneficiaries |
| Partnership | Partnership name, registration, all partners' identities |

## Red Flags During CIP

- Document appears altered, forged, or inconsistent
- Customer reluctant to provide required identification
- Identification document is expired or about to expire
- Photo does not match the individual presenting it
- Address cannot be verified or matches a high-risk location
- Customer uses a P.O. Box with no other verifiable address
- Multiple customers share the same identification details
